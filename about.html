THE REAL STORY: Why I Left Corporate

For 15 years, I was inside Fortune 500s. CTO-level roles. Building AI systems from scratch.

My job? Solve problems nobody else could.

And I got good at it.

A telecom giant with 11M in preventable downtime? I built the system that prevented it.

A logistics company bleeding ₹2Cr annually on route optimization? Problem solved.

But here’s what broke for me:

I couldn’t teach it.

Corporate policy: I wasn’t allowed to share the framework. Wasn’t allowed to work with external clients. Wasn’t allowed to teach what I’d learned.

I was sitting on a system that worked 50+ times. And I couldn’t help the people who needed it most.

One day, a VP from a competitor reached out: “I heard you solve this. Can you help us? We’re about to spend ₹50L with a consultant and have no idea if it’ll work.”

I said: “I’d love to, but my company won’t allow it.”

That moment changed everything.

I realized: The gap isn’t in AI technology. The gap is that leaders have no clarity on which problems AI can actually solve.

So they hire consultants for ₹100K+ and spend 6 months finding out.

I decided to fix that gap.

I left corporate. Built a framework that teaches leaders to diagnose AI opportunities themselves. Tested it with 50+ professionals across automotive, retail, finance, logistics.

Same result every time: Clarity. Confidence. Execution.

Healthcare solving automotive problems. Retail solving manufacturing challenges. Mathematical patterns from physics applied to business operations.

Most experts stay in their domain. They look straight ahead. I learned to look sideways.

That’s why I can diagnose any AI opportunity in 1 hour.

Not because I know everything. But because I’ve seen the same patterns 50+ times across different industries. I can spot what fits and what doesn’t.
